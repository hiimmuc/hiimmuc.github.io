**Duration:** Nov 2023 – Sep 2025  
**Organization:** Human Robotics Laboratory, NAIST · Ikoma, Japan  
**Collaboration:** Hitachi Corp, Japan

## Overview

Designed and built a high-fidelity Virtual Reality driving simulator using Unreal Engine, integrated with an AI driving assistant. The system is used to train drivers and evaluate driver cognition in controlled, reproducible virtual environments — a collaboration between NAIST's Human Robotics Laboratory and Hitachi Corporation.

## Key Contributions

- Developed the simulation environment in **Unreal Engine** with realistic driving scenarios and vehicle physics
- Integrated an **AI driving assistant** that monitors driver behavior, provides real-time feedback, and evaluates cognitive load
- Designed data collection and logging pipelines for driving performance metrics and physiological indicators
- Collaborated with Hitachi Corp engineers on system integration and evaluation protocols

## Technologies

`Unreal Engine` `Python` `AI/ML` `VR` `ROS 2` `Human-Robot Interaction` `Driver Cognition`
